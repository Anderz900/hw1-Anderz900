Good Visualizations

Visualization #1:

Shareholders in the company might be interested in investing more...

The most interested audience would be shareholders in Tesla and workers in Tesla who are inclined to see their own company prosper. Of course, the rest of the audience are people who are interested in investing in either stocks, or perhaps a new Tesla car. This graph conveys to the most interested that despite a tough start, revenues are beginning to grow exponentially. Tesla is beginning to be a dependable corporation for technology and will continue to have a presence in future markets.

Length and color

The length of the bars encode the revenue (quantitative) made by Tesla over one year. The color of blue and green encode and distinguishes the revenue and loss (categorical).

Revenue vs Losses

In this easily analyzable graph, readers will be looking at the lengths of the bars to compare the revenues and losses of each year. They will also take into account the proportion of loss and revenue each year which is made apparent by nicely having losses, a term associated with a value being decreased, be extended below into the negative space (below the x-axis).

Truthful, functional, clean, and insightful

As the numbers do not lie nor are there any scaling delusions, this is an honest graph. Since a bar graph is very easy to read and conveys a pretty direct message, the graph is functional. The fact that there is not any other decorations make the graph simple and clean, giving some element of beauty. More importantly, the insightful message transmitted will prove useful for the stated members of the audience who can be more positive with their future investments in technology, especially in Tesla. It could even be enlightening given the growing importance of technology. Due to all these factors, this visualization is indeed a good one.

Visualization #3:

Apple customers

The audience is mainly apple customers, especially those who own smartphones, and secondary would be any smartphone owner. The graphs show the increasing price of the iPhone, and other smartphones. By comparing all this data, the message is that Apple is beginning to stop profiting from the sale of iPhones, and now intend to profit more from their services.

Grid-like strucutre

The first graph has a grid-like background where vertical and horizontal lines encode different data by position. The first graph's vertical lines encode the price (quantitative) of the iPhone, and the horizontal lines encode the years (ordinal) and iPhone version that has been released. Also, the different colored dots in the grid are different versions (because of their storage capacity) of the iPhones released that year (categorical). The second graph's horizontal lines encode the revenue (quantitative) made by the different companies per smartphone. The x-axis shows the year(ordinal) so as to present the revenue over time. The different colors of the lines encode the different companies (categorical). In the last graph the horizontal lines encode the smartphone shipments (quantitative) worldwide in billions and once again the years can be perceived in the x-axis (ordinal). The different colors show the different companies (categorical).

Multiple perspectives

The fact that there are multiple graphs instead of one makes it very easy to read. On that note, the dots that represent the different storage capacities facilitate the year by year comparison as one moves down the grid. It is also easy to differentiate the extremes in prices. The lines on the second graph allow one to catch trends in each companies' history. Being able to be differentiated by color disallows confusion with identifying the companies as well. The last graph's use of different colors make it easy to compare the proportions of Apple's shipments with everybody else. As one get to the current years, Apple's decreased smartphone shipments reflects the message of the visualization.

Clear, functional, enlightening, truthful

In terms of beauty, the amount of decorations is kept varied and judiciously used; the clarity of the graphs is thereby highlighted. The use of three different graphs allow one to receive multiple perspectives, attributing the visualization a functional quality. Also, because the graphs' data agree with each other and the source of the data comes from the companies' own reports, the graphs are truthful. The price of mobile devices has long plagued customers with their prices, but this report could prove fruitul for future interested customers. The graph is therefore enlightening and could be a great hint for them. All these reasons considered, the visualization is a great one.

Bad Visualizations

Visualization #1:

Litterers beware...

This visualization is mainly intended for people who do not recycle their water bottles, but it can also be directed towards environmmental activists. The image clearly depicts the wasteful and corruptive culture of today's world and attempts to convince the viewer into better habits before more damage is done.

Qualitative somehow measures quantitative?

The only data that is encoded is the color blue coming from the piled up water bottles (categorical) in order to represent the water bottles, and the sheer amount of bottles encode the amount of purchased water bottles (quantitatve) around the world on a daily basis. The amount is portrayed by comparing the quantity of bottles with the size of the Eiffel Tower.

Inward or outward projection?

Since there are not many objects or variables in the image, it is relatively lax on the number of things to keep track of. But, the fact that it is a 3D image makes the comparison difficult to perceive. Since the stack of bottles is behind the Eiffel Tower, there could be error in perception as one has to not only compare the height of the water bottle stack, but also it's distance off in the background which could skew results. The resulting comparions makes it very difficult to read.

Reputable source, beautiful 3D rendering and detail, enlightening, but not very functional...

The data comes from a reliable source, and the portrayal next to the Eiffel Tower seems accurate as well. Since there are no scales, there is less opportunity to be dishonest. Therefore this is an honest representation of the message. It is also beautiful as our eyes only focus on the two objects that have considerable detail. It is also important to know this fact as this corresponds to a global dilemma therefore it is enlightening. However, the fact there is no scale definitely takes functionality out of the picture. To have a better comparison, there should be a scale comparing the height at the very least while still retaining simplicity. In addition, while the message is clear, the representation of that message fails to be insightful due to stack and Eiffel Tower not being exactly next to one another. This could cause confusion and make someone arrive at a different conclusion. In other words, if this were art then it would score highly, but as a visualziation it fails in too many areas and is therefore a bad one.

Visualization #2:

US voters

The audience is going to mainly be all residents living in the US, especially voters. The trend in the graph shows that most notable candidates eventually will be somewhat liked by the populace. This visualization could prove important as it can help distinguish who among them will have the higher chance of being elected President.

Before and after debate

The blue dots in the graph encode the names (categorical) of the candidates. Both x and y axes encode how favorable (quantitave) the candidate is before and after debate respectively.

But what is the cumulative effect?

If readers want to see how favorable a candidate was before or after debate, then one only needed to see where the blue dot is in the x or y axis which is easy to do. However, when one is trying to make a cumulative comparison, there is not a direct comparison and if a candidate is closer to the top right then one can say the candidate is popular in both aspects. Nevertheless, it can be hard to ascertain that and there is potential for confusion as it is not straightforward.

Trustworthy, somewhat inaccurate, pretty patterns

Since the data was accumulated through a survey, the data source is trustworthy and the graph also reflects the data. Therefore, it is truthful. However, while impressions can accurately be graphed both before and after debate, there is no clear cumulative effect of this, it is murky and therefore the graph is not optimal. Yet, like a radial graph, it has an element of beauty as a pattern seems to arise. The graph also gets interesting information that can prove useful in the political field, thereby making it insightful and enlightening. All things considered, since functionality is hugely important, this visualization fails to live up to it's idea.

Visualization #2:

More numbers, less words

Pre-debate favorability was encoded with a vertical axis (position) on the left-hand side ranging from -1 to 1 (quantitative). Debate peformance was recorded with another vertical axis (position) but on the right-hand side with the same range as the last one (quantitative).

More lines, less points

I completely changed the design, yet the simple aspect is still retained. I decided to represent the cadidates' represenation of impression with lines instead of points. I would gather the data from the previous graph, plot the points accordingly on the axes, and connect the lines.

Massive improvement

I think my visualization is a vast improvement from the original. The first one had the characteristics of a radial graph, making it hard to see how two or more factors jointly affected the overall result. As there are only two factors of significant consideration, I believed calculating the slope between them by simply doing the substraction of the second axis value from the first one gives one an accurate representation of how the impression changed. My graph also takes into account how valuable their contribution to the debate is with respect to their pre-debate favorability. In other words, a semi-bad peformance on debate by a well-respected candidate leaves a larger impression on the crowd than an even worse peformance by a much less known candidate. The higher the impression ratio, the better the impression the candidate gave off. In other words, the higher the slope, the better the impression. With that logic, the best impression was given off by O'Rourke (-0.23) and the worst was Sanders (-0.7). It also makes sense that all these ratios are negative as the general trend should be that we are more impressed with the popularity of the candidate then when actually faced with their arguments on debate day.

Trust the process...

While there are many intersecting lines (a bit more messy) in my redesign, this allows us to better understand each candidate's overrall impression throughout the process and we can compare these changes by visualizing the lines, and slopes. Honestly, my graph does not have any trade-offs other than that. The new visualization is a great improvement.
